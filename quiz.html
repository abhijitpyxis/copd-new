Asthma and COPD have symptoms in common- such as shortness of breath, coughing and wheezing- but they are two distinct lung diseases.

If you have COPD, you need to take extra precautions to prevent contracting any respiratory illness, such as Flu and Pneumonia, since it adds on to the complications.

It's never too late to quit smoking. The damage to your lungs from years of smoking can never be completely reversed, but if you quit smoking anytime it may slow the progression of COPD symptoms.

Yes. A lung-function test, known as spirometry,can confirm if you have COPD.

COPD is not just a man's disease. Reports from Amercian Lung Association suggest that, women are also at a high risk to get COPD.

COPD is a chronic disease, meaning it is a long-term condition. But there have been great advances in COPD medicines which can often control symptoms and slow the progression, espically if it's diagonsed in the early stages.

Smoking is one of the most common causes of COPD. But years of breathing second hand smoke or working in an area where you're exposed to airborne chemicals, dust & other particles can raise your risk for COPD

It is true that about 3 out of 4 COPD cases are diagnosed in people above 55, but COPD can be found in people of younger age groups as well.

Regular exercise (Proper exercise and breathing techniques) expands lung capacity and helps relieve COPD-related fatigue.
