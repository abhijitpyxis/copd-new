Nutrition
for COPD patients

Your body uses food as fuel for all of its activities. No single food will supply all the nutrients you need - a healthy diet has lots of variety. For patients with COPD and other chronic lung conditions, maintaining a healthy lifestyle is the key for managing their condition and improving symptoms. Moderate exercise along with following a healthy diet will help reduce the tenderness.

Nutrition Concerns

  • Malnutrition (lack of proper nutrition)
  • Weight gain or weight loss

    Do you really feel tired with lack of energy throughout the day?

    Helpful tips

  • Prepare simple and easy food items and take help of your family friends for food preparation.

    • Do you feel a heavy stomach and bloated any time?

    Helpful tips

  • Eat several small meals throughout day.
  • Drink fluids 1 hour before or after meals.
  • Avoid foods that cause gas and bloating like cabbage, cauliflower, tur dal, watermelons, radish, chana, besan, beans and lentils, carrots, prunes and apricots.
  • lncrease your fiber intake by eating more of fruits and vegetables to avoid constipation.

    • Do you feel a heavy stomach and bloated any time?

    Helpful tips

  • Try not to rush meals and eat less fried and foods with high fat content.
  • Eat several small meals throughout day. Drink fluids 1 hour before or after meals.
  • Avoid foods that cause gas and bloating like cabbage, cauliflower, tur dal, watermelons, radish, chana, besan, beans and lentils, carrots, prunes and apricots.

    • Helpful tips

  • Continue having small frequent meals to avoid bloating.
    • Have you observed that you have put on weight in the past few months or post starting with the treatment?

    Helpful tips

  • Lose weight gradually and sensibly, consult your doctor or dietician.
  • Become more active by doing daily exercise for 25 mins (could be twice a day).
  • Eat nutrient-dense, balanced diet.
  • Monitor or record food intake on daily basis so as to lose weight.
